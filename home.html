The Indian Institutes of Technology Joint Entrance Examination (IIT-JEE) is a competitive exam used as the primary means of admission to undergraduate engineering programs at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) as well as a few other engineering schools in India. The exam is typically taken by students in their final year of high school and covers topics such as physics, chemistry, and mathematics. The exam is considered to be one of the most difficult in the world, with a low acceptance rate. The IIT-JEE was replaced by JEE(Main) and JEE(Advanced) from 2013. JEE(Main) is the first stage of the two-phase JEE, JEE(Advanced) is the second and final stage of the IITs.
The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is a competitive exam in India used to determine eligibility for admission to undergraduate medical and dental programs. The exam is primarily intended for students who have completed their 12th grade education, and covers subjects such as physics, chemistry, and biology. NEET is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) annually usually in the month of May. It is a single entrance exam for all Medical/Dental colleges, except AIIMS and JIPMER. The NEET score is used by all medical and dental colleges in India except the All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) and Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education and Research (JIPMER) which have their own entrance exams. The NEET has been made mandatory for students who wish to pursue their medical studies in India from the academic session 2021-22.
